= pBTC Go-To-Market Plan

This document defines a practical plan to grow pBTC adoption while keeping
technical transparency and user trust central.

== Objectives

Primary goals for the next 90 days:

- Increase qualified awareness of pBTC on Pulsechain
- Improve bridge-start to bridge-complete conversion
- Grow repeat usage and partner integrations
- Maintain transparent messaging about TBTC-powered infrastructure

== Positioning and narrative

Core position:

- `pBTC` is the Pulsechain-branded user experience
- It is powered by TBTC bridge infrastructure and operator security

Messaging pillars:

- Trust through transparency (contracts, audits, explorer links)
- Utility (use BTC liquidity in Pulsechain DeFi)
- Simplicity (clear bridge flow and status visibility)
- Reliability (observable uptime and incident communication)

== Audience segments

=== Retail BTC holders (first-time bridgers)

Needs:

- Safety explanations in plain language
- Step-by-step bridge walkthrough
- Clear fee and timing expectations

Best channels:

- Short educational videos
- X threads and visuals
- Wallet/community partner guides

=== DeFi power users

Needs:

- Liquidity depth and integrations
- Real-time metrics (TVL, volume, completion rate)
- Advanced transparency page with contract details

Best channels:

- DEX and protocol partner announcements
- Technical blog posts
- Community AMAs

=== Integrators and ecosystem teams

Needs:

- Stable API/SDK and docs
- Integration runbook and support path
- Co-marketing opportunities

Best channels:

- Direct BD outreach
- Integration playbooks
- Joint launch content

== Trust and compliance guardrails

- Avoid guaranteed-return language or profit promises.
- Avoid wording that can be interpreted as investment advice.
- Always disclose protocol dependencies where relevant: `Powered by TBTC infrastructure`.
- Ensure addresses, symbols, and explorer links match on-chain values.
- Keep risk disclosures current on app, docs, and campaign landing pages.

== 90-day rollout plan

=== Phase 1 (Weeks 1-3): credibility foundation

- Publish a public transparency page (contracts, audits, security process)
- Publish beginner bridge guide with screenshots and FAQ
- Add status/uptime panel links to pBTC portal and docs
- Launch one technical deep-dive article on bridge safety model

Success signals:

- Lower support questions per 100 bridge starts
- Higher completion rate for first-time bridge attempts

=== Phase 2 (Weeks 4-7): acquisition campaigns

- Run a "First Bridge" campaign with a clear, low-friction onboarding flow
- Coordinate KOL/community AMAs focused on safety + use cases
- Launch partner co-marketing with at least 2 Pulsechain ecosystem apps
- Publish weekly social proof dashboard snapshots (TVL, users, completion)

Success signals:

- Increased weekly bridge starts
- Increased landing-page to wallet-connect conversion

=== Phase 3 (Weeks 8-12): retention and ecosystem scale

- Add lifecycle messaging: "what to do next with pBTC" in app/docs
- Run re-engagement campaign for users with incomplete bridge attempts
- Publish integration case studies with partners
- Expand referral/community champion program

Success signals:

- Improved 7-day and 30-day repeat-usage rates
- Increased partner-originated bridge volume

== Channel strategy

- Owned: docs, portal, blog, changelog, Discord
- Earned: partner announcements, community spaces, media mentions
- Social: X, Telegram, Discord updates with consistent weekly cadence
- Field/community: AMAs, office hours, integration workshops

Cadence recommendation:

- 3-5 social posts per week
- 1 educational long-form post per week
- 1 live community session every 2 weeks

== Campaign content system

Use a repeatable content mix each week:

- One educational piece (how bridge works)
- One proof piece (metrics/uptime/security progress)
- One use-case story (what users can do with pBTC)
- One product update (new integration, fix, or UX improvement)

== KPI framework

Track weekly and monthly:

- Awareness: landing sessions, social reach, share of voice
- Activation: wallet connects, bridge starts
- Conversion: bridge completion rate, time-to-completion
- Retention: 7-day and 30-day repeat bridge users
- Ecosystem: number of active integrations, partner-sourced volume
- Trust: support ticket rate, incident count, mean time to communication

== Reporting and ownership

Suggested owners:

- Product marketing lead: campaign calendar and messaging
- Developer relations lead: integration content and technical AMAs
- Operations/reliability lead: trust metrics and incident communication

Weekly operating rhythm:

- Monday: KPI review + campaign planning
- Wednesday: content and partner sync
- Friday: retrospective + next-week adjustments
